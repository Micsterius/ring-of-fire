Explanation and General Rules

Texas holdem is one of the most commonly played variants of the card game poker. Each round consists of 4 phases in which the player has to decide whether he/she wants to continue participating in the round or opt out (fold).

Rounds

Round 1

Each player receives 2 cards, which he/she holds face down in front of the other players. Now the players have to decide whether: You want to participate in the game round. In addition, there is a player who pays the minimum contribution for the Participation of the round. This player is called Big Blind. In the first round, he was the last to win the Possibility act. Depending on the betting behavior of all players, all players can also make the decision several times. meeting must decide whether they want to continue participating in the round.

Examples

Example 1

Player 1 sets the minimum amount of 10 (call).
Player 2 exit game round. (fold)
Player 3 as a small blind already had to bet 5 chips. Now he called and has to go to 10 fill.
Player 4 as a big blind has already set 10 and can Now decide whether he/she confirmed (checked) or wants to increase it again. If he/she increases, the other players will be asked if you increase or want to follow suit for the same amount.
The end of the round is reached as soon as only 1 player participates or all players set the same amount.

Player 2

Player 1 sets an amount of 20, doubling the bet (raise).
Player 2 exit game round. (fold)
Player 3 as a small blind already had to bet 5 chips. Nevertheless exit game round (fold).
Player 4 as a big blind has already set 10 and can now decide whether he/she goes with you bet 10 to set in total 20 (call), wants to increase again (re-raise) or gets out (fold).
The end of the round is reached as soon as only 1 player participates or all players set the same amount.

Beispiel 3

Player 1 exit game round. (fold)
Player 2 exit game round. (fold)
Player 3 as a small blind already had to bet 5 chips. Nevertheless exit game round (fold).
Player 4 automatically wins as the last remaining player.

Round 2

In addition to your own 2 cards, 3 cards are now laid face up. This is called a flop. The Players now check whether their own cards with the 3 additional cards allow a good winning combination. Accordingly is set. Basically, a bluff is also possible in every round. Due to the setting behavior, you also signal to the players whether you have a high or low probability of winning for yourself sees.

Round 3

In addition to the 3 cards face up, another card is laid. This is called Turn. Now the Players again decide one by one whether they want to bet money or not.

Round 4

In addition to the 4 cards face up, another card is laid. This is called River. This is the last round. Now all remaining players bet until all have deposited the same amount or there is only one player left.
From your own 2 cards and the 5 cards face up, 5 cards are now selected, which are the best hand of the player. This can include 2, 1 or no card of the players hand. The player with the best 5 cards wins.

Rating of poker hands

High Card

If you do not achieve another rating, the highest card will be considered. If none of the other players a better combination, the one with the highest card wins. The lowest value is the 2 and the highest the ace.

Pair

A pair means that a player has 2 cards of the same value. (e.g. Queen of Hearts and Queen of Spades)

Two Pairs

Two pairs means that a player has 2 times two cards of the same value. (e.g. Queen of Hearts, Queen of Spades, Cross 8 and Heart 8)

Triplet

A triplet means that a player has 3 cards of the same value. (e.g. Heart 8, Cross 8 and Spades 8)

Straight

A straight means that a player has 5 consecutive cards. In this case, the ace can be the first or the last card. A jump over the ace is not possible. Therefore, King, Ace, 2, 3, 4 would not be valid. The Colors don't matter

Flush

In a flush, the player has 5 cards of the same suit.

Full House

In a full house, the player has a pair and a triplet.

Quadruplet

A quadruple means that a player has 4 cards of the same value. (e.g. Heart 8, Cross 8, Diamond 8 and Spades 8)

Straight Flush

A straight flush means that a player has a street where all 5 cards have the same suit (e.g. Spades 7, Spades 8, Spades 9, Spades 10 and Jack of Spades)

Royal Flush

A royal flush means that a player has the highest road where all 5 cards are the same suit (e.g. Spades 10, Jack of Spades, Queen of Spades, King Spades and Ace of Spades)
